Carbonara

Description

Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish made with simple, high-quality ingredients. Traditionally, it features spaghetti tossed with a creamy sauce made from eggs, grated Pecorino Romano cheese, pancetta (or guanciale), and freshly ground black pepper. The heat from the cooked pasta gently cooks the eggs, creating a rich, silky coating without the need for cream.

Ingredients

  • 200g (7 oz) spaghetti
  • 100g (3.5 oz) pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • 1 large egg
  • 50g (1.75 oz) Pecorino Romano cheese, finely grated
  • Freshly ground black pepper (to taste)
  • Salt (for pasta water)

Steps

  1. Boil pasta in salted water until al dente.
  2. Cook pancetta in a pan until crispy.
  3. Whisk eggs and cheese together in a bowl; add black pepper.
  4. Drain pasta, reserving some cooking water.
  5. Combine hot pasta with pancetta, then remove from heat.
  6. Quickly mix in egg mixture, adding a splash of pasta water if needed for creaminess.
  7. Serve immediately, topped with extra cheese and pepper.
